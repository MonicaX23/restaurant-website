Our story

A small kitchen, an open table

Olivetta started with a market basket and not much of a plan — cook what's fresh, keep it simple, and make room for everyone who walks in.

Cooking close to the season

We're a neighbourhood place, not a chain. The list is short because we shop small and often, and we'd rather cook two tomatoes well than ten badly. When something's at its best, it goes on the board; when it's gone, it's gone until next year.

The room is built for the way people actually eat — quick weekday lunches, unhurried dinners, and the occasional table of twelve that turns into the best night of the week. Children are welcome, dietary needs are easy, and nobody's rushed out the door.
